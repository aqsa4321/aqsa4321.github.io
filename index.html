Biography

Hi, I'm Aqsa Yousaf, a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Texas at Arlington, where I'm affiliated with the Cyber-Physical System Security Lab working under the supervision of Dr. Habeeb Olufowobi. My research bridges the domains of medical imaging, explainable AI, and 3D scene reconstruction, with a focus on developing transparent and robust AI systems for healthcare applications.


Before beginning my Ph.D., I earned my M.S. in Computer Science from the Pakistan Institute of Engineering and Applied Sciences (PIEAS) and a B.Sc. in Electrical Engineering from the University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore. I have also worked as a Computer Vision Engineer at Evercam, where I built super-resolution pipelines for large-scale video analytics, and as a Research Assistant at Computer Laboratory of Ambient and Wearable Systems, where I developed machine learning models for wearable health monitoring systems.


Currently, my research explores interpretable deep learning for disease detection, generative 3D reconstruction using Gaussian splatting, and optimization techniques for sparse-view CT imaging. I have also contributed to projects on XAI evaluation in clinical decision support, saliency map benchmarking, and multi-modal signal processing.


In my spare time, I enjoy creative writing, designing visuals and exploring new AI tools.

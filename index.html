Biography

I'm a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science department at the University of Texas at Arlington. My primary research interests include medical imaging, explainable AI, and 3D scene reconstruction. My work spans across computer vision, including object detection (RT-DETR, DINO-DETR, YOLO), generative models (GANs), and Gaussian splatting for 3D reconstruction, as well as sensor signal processing for wearable healthcare systems.


Before joining the PhD program, I worked for several years as a computer vision engineer and research assistant where I gathered practical experiences in AI-driven solutions, machine learning, and computer vision. I completed my Master's in Computer Science from Pakistan Institute of Engineering & Applied Sciences and my Bachelor's in Electrical Engineering from University of Engineering and Technology, Lahore.
